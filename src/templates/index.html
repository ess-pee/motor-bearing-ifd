Intelligent Motor Fault Detection

By: Shaurya Pathak

About This Demo

Bearing fault detection isn't just some academic exercise—it's a real-world necessity that keeps industries like manufacturing, aerospace, and energy from grinding to a halt. When bearings fail without warning, you're looking at catastrophic breakdowns, millions in downtime costs, and even safety risks; in a country like China, yearly estimates pegged maintanance costs of aricraft engines due to bearing failure at over $10 billion. Catching these faults early through predictive maintenance can save a lot of money and lives, but most traditional methods are stuck in the past, relying on complex signal processing that eats up time and resources. For real-time monitoring, especially on edge devices where every second counts, we need something smarter, faster, and more practical.
My work builds on the 1D CNN architecture that delivers >98% accuracy on raw vibrational signals across datasets like CWRU, Mehran Triaxial, and MAFAULDA, without relying on short-time fourier transforms or wavelet transforms that most state-of-the-art models lean on. Those methods add 10-100 ms of preprocessing overhead, but my model skips that entirely, learning fault patterns straight from the data, making it perfect for edge deployment in industrial IoT.
It also demonstrates promise with transfer learning, averaging 77% accuracy with just 1% of the data, beating training from scratch on average by 6%. This potentially enables lab-trained models to be deployed directly on edge devices, with minimal retraining. Compared to the bulkier models out there, this one keeps it simple and effective, suggesting much of the computational overhead can be bypassed for real-time fault detection.
Please do visit the GitHub repository for this project for a more detailed description of the model architecture, training process, and results.
